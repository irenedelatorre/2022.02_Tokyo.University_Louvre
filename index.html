Studying visitors movement's using WiFi Tracking data

A promenade through the Louvre

WiFi tracking is a valuable tool used in various settings to understand pedestrian behavior. This research employed WiFi tracking at the Louvre Museum for three months, from November 2017 to January 2018, to obtain the location of visitors and make some observations.

Considerations

It is important to consider the following problems regarding WiFi data:

  • The WiFi coverage area is large, and the boundary is vague. Sometimes people in the rooms next door are caught.
  • Some areas are not covered. For example, room 711, where the Mona Lisa is displayed, does not have a Wi-Fi access point.
  • The device settings, such as having the wireless option enabled or disabled, affects the result.
  • People with multiple devices connected to the Wi-Fi are counted twice.
